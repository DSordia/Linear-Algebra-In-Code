  • If elimination requires row exchanges, then A = LU becomes PA = LU, where P is the matrix that exchanges the rows of A
  • Recall: A permutation matrix P is the identity matrix with reordered rows, the number of permutations of an n x n matrix is n!, and P-1 = PT which implies PTP = I
  • Transpose: Switching rows and columns of a matrix - (AT)ij = Aji
  • Symmetric matrices: A = AT (transposing does not change the matrix)
  • Let R be a rectangular matrix, then RT * R is always symmetric
  • (RT*R)T = RT * RTT = RT * R
  • Vector space: a space consisting of column vectors in which the resulting column vector of adding any column vector to a scalar multiple of another column vector is also within the vector space. In other words, a space which is closed under addition and scalar multiplication.
  • Subspace: A space within a vector space which is also closed under addition and scalar multiplication, and contains the zero vector.
  • Subspaces of R2: All of R2, any line that goes through the zero vector, and the zero vector itself
  • The columns of a matrix A and all of their linear combinations forms a subspace called the Column Space C(A)

    # LECTURE 5 - Transposes, Permutations, Spaces R^n
     
    import numpy as np
     
    # Showing P inverse = P transpose and P transpose * P = I
     
    I = np.identity(3)
    P = np.random.permutation(I)
     
    print(f'I = \n{I}\nPermutation of I = \n{P}\nInverse of permutation = \n{np.round(np.linalg.inv(P), 2)}\nTranspose of permutation = \n{P.transpose()}\n'
    f'P transpose * P = \n{np.dot(P.transpose(), P)}')
     
    #OUTPUT:
    '''

    [[1. 0. 0.]
     [0. 1. 0.]
     [0. 0. 1.]]
    Permutation of I =
    [[0. 1. 0.]

     [0. 0. 1.]]
    Inverse of permutation =
    [[0. 1. 0.]
     [1. 0. 0.]
     [0. 0. 1.]]
    Transpose of permutation =
    [[0. 1. 0.]
     [1. 0. 0.]
     [0. 0. 1.]]
    P transpose * P =
    [[1. 0. 0.]
     [0. 1. 0.]
