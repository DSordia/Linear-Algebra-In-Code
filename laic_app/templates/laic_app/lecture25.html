  • Symmetric matrices: A = AT
  • For symmetric matrices, the product of the diagonal values is equal to the determinant
  • For real symmetric matrices, the eigenvalues are real and the eigenvectors are orthogonal (if there is a repeated eigenvalue, then the eigenvectors can be chosen to be orthogonal) - scale the magnitude to 1 and they become orthonormal
  • Recall that usually we have A = SΛS-1, however with symmetric matrices we have A = QΛQT where Q's columns are the orthonormal eigenvectors of A
  • For symmetric matrices, the number of positive pivots is equal to the number of positive eigenvalues and the number of negative pivots is equal to the number of negative eigenvalues
  • Positive definite matrix: symmetric matrices where all eigenvalues (and thus pivots) are positive
  • The determinant and all sub-determinants of positive definite matrices are positive

    # Showing that the eigenvectors of a symmetric matrix are orthogonal
     
    A = np.array([[1, 2, 3], [2, 4, 5], [3, 5, 8]])
    evalues, evectors = np.linalg.eig(A)

    # Showing that A = QΛQT
     
    A = np.array([[1, 2, 3], [2, 4, 5], [3, 5, 8]])
    evalues, evectors = np.linalg.eig(A)
     
    capitalLamda = np.dot(np.linalg.inv(evectors), np.dot(A, evectors))
    Q = []
    for i in range(0, len(evectors)):
        evectors[i] = evectors[i] / np.linalg.norm(evectors[i])
        Q.append(evectors[i])
     
    capitalLamda = np.array(capitalLamda)
    Q = np.array(Q)
     
    print(f'A = \n{A}\nQ * capital lamda * Q tranpose = \n{np.dot(Q, np.dot(capitalLamda, Q.transpose()))}')
     
    #OUTPUT:
    '''
    A =
    [[1 2 3]
     [2 4 5]
     [3 5 8]]
    Q * capital lamda * Q tranpose =
    [[1. 2. 3.]
     [2. 4. 5.]
     [3. 5. 8.]]
    '''
     
    # Eigenvalues of a positive definite matrix
     
    A = np.array([[5, 2], [2, 3]])
    evalues, evectors = np.linalg.eig(A)
     
    print(f'Positive definite matrix A = \n{A}\nDeterminant of A = {np.round(np.linalg.det(A))}\neigenvalues of A = {evalues})'
    f'\nproduct of eigenvalues = {round(evalues[0] * evalues[1])}')

    ''' #eigenvalues are positive and real as expected
