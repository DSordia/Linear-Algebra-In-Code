  • The Four Fundamental Subspaces: Column space C(A), Null space N(A), Row space R(A), Left null space (Null space N(AT))
  • Row space: all combinations of the rows of a matrix A. The rows form a basis of the row space if the rows are all independent. The row space is also all combinations of the columns of AT (C(AT))
  • Take an m x n matrix A. the Column space is in Rm, the null space is in Rn, the row space is in Rn, the left null space is in Rm
  • Dimension of column space = dimension of row space = rank, dimension of null space = n - r, dimension of left null space = m - r
  • Basis for column space = pivot columns, basis for row space = first r rows of rref(A)
  • Basis for left null space: take the identity matrix and apply all operations on it that were applied to get from A to rref(A) the basis is the row(s) of this altered identity matrix that when multiplied with A generates the zero vector (which combinations of rows generates the null space, unlike the basis for the regular null space which is the combination of columns that generates the null space)

    # LECTURE 10 - The Four Fundamental Subspaces
     
    import numpy as np

    # The four fundamental subspaces of a matrix
     
    A = Matrix([[1, 2, 3, 1], [1, 1, 2, 1], [1, 2, 3, 1]])
     
    print(f'A = \n{A}\nColumn space of A = \n{A.columnspace()}\nNull space of A = \n{A.nullspace()}\nRow space of A = \n{A.T.columnspace()}\nLeft null space = \n{A.T.nullspace()}')
